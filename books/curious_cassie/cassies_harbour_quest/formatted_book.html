Science Words We Learned:

- Adaptation: Special features that help an animal survive in its particular environment

- Invertebrate: An animal without a backbone or internal skeleton

- Camouflage: Colours or patterns that help an animal blend into its surroundings and hide from predators

- Symbiotic relationship: When two different species help each other survive

- Ecosystem: A community where all living things interact and depend on each other

- Food web: The interconnected feeding relationships in an ecosystem

Create Your Own Mini Ecosystem

‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT: Ask an adult to help with this experiment!

You'll need: A large glass jar with a lid, aquarium gravel or small pebbles, aged tap water (leave tap water sitting for 24 hours so chlorine evaporates), small aquatic plants from a pet shop, a few small pond snails or aquatic insects from an aquarium store

Instructions:

1. Wash your hands before and after handling aquarium materials

2. Add a layer of clean pebbles (about 2-3 cm deep) to your jar

3. Carefully pour in aged water until the jar is about three-quarters full

4. Gently add your aquatic plants, pressing their roots into the gravel

5. Add your snails or insects

6. Place your mini ecosystem in a spot with indirect sunlight (not direct sun, which gets too hot)

7. Observe over several weeks!

What to watch for:

- Plants producing oxygen bubbles (photosynthesis!)

- Snails eating algae that grows on the glass and plants

- Waste from animals providing nutrients for plants

- The whole system balancing itself!

üåä CRITICAL REMINDERS:

- NEVER take creatures from rock pools or natural habitats‚Äîthis harms wild populations

- Only use animals from pet shops that are bred for aquariums

- NEVER release pets or aquarium creatures into natural waterways‚Äîthey can harm local ecosystems

- If you observe rock pools in nature, look but don't touch, and never remove anything

- Always wash hands after handling aquarium materials
